About Me

Hi, I'm Lorenzo Ortiz, an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) currently residing in Yakima, WA. I play a crucial role in providing timely and quality care to patients in various emergency situations. My experience in electronic medical records enhances the efficiency and accuracy of emergency response and documentation.

In addition to my work in the medical field, I'm a Software Engineering student at Western Governors University, driven by a passion for problem-solving and technology. With a strong background in military medicine, having served as a combat medic in the US Army for four years, I've received multiple honors for leadership, medical preparedness, and coordination skills during deployments to Korea and assignments at Okubo Medical Clinic.

I am eager to apply my diverse experience and technical skills to the software engineering field. I value teamwork, learning, and innovation, and I am always open to new challenges and opportunities. Currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering at Western Governors University, I am authorized to work in the US for any employer.

Learning Ethical Hacking: As part of my ongoing journey, I am also delving into the realm of ethical hacking, exploring the intersection of technology, security, and problem-solving.
